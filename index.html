01 — About

Compiler engineer by day, document-AI researcher by training.

I'm a Senior Compiler Engineer at Quadric.io, where I work on the Chimera Graph Compiler (CGC) — a compiler built on Apache TVM that lowers trained neural networks into fast, C++-programmable code for Quadric's Chimera GPNPU, a hybrid CPU + DSP + NPU processor for edge AI.

I recently completed my MS by Research at IIT Bombay (CSE), advised by Prof. Ganesh Ramakrishnan and Prof. Parag Chaudhuri. My research makes text-rich documents in Indian languages machine-readable — spanning handwritten text recognition, table structure recognition, weakly-supervised text detection and multi-granular visual grounding, with work published at WACV and ICDAR.

Earlier I was a Software Development Engineer at Amazon (Digital Payments) and a Data Science Associate at ZS Associates — and in August 2026 I'm returning to Amazon as an SDE II on the MENA Payments team in Bengaluru, working on securing payment experiences for Amazon India. I hold a B.E. in Computer Science from BITS Pilani, Hyderabad, and I've delivered OCR/HTR model APIs to Bhashini, the Government of India's national language-technology mission.

I like problems that live between systems and intelligence — compilers, high-performance ML, and AI for real-world documents — and I care about writing things that ship and hold up.

03 — Research & Publications

Making India's documents machine-readable.

Four papers on document AI — text detection, table structure, handwritten OCR and visual grounding for text-rich, multilingual documents. Advised by Prof. Ganesh Ramakrishnan & Prof. Parag Chaudhuri, IIT Bombay. Google Scholar ↗

Preprint '25 arXiv 10 cites

PLATTER: A Page-Level Handwritten Text Recognition System for Indic Scripts

Badri Vishal Kasuba, Dhruv Kudale, Venkatapathy Subramanian, Parag Chaudhuri, Ganesh Ramakrishnan · first author

An end-to-end, page-level handwritten OCR framework for 10 Indic languages that pairs language-agnostic word-level text detection with recognition, benchmarks six HTR models, and releases CHIPS, a curated corpus of handwritten Indic scripts.

arXiv PDF Code Semantic Scholar
Preprint '25 arXiv newest

DRISHTIKON: Multi-Granular Visual Grounding for Text-Rich Document Images

Badri Vishal Kasuba, Parag Chaudhuri, Ganesh Ramakrishnan · first author

A multi-granular visual-grounding framework that pinpoints exactly where an answer lives in a text-rich, multilingual document — at the block, line, word and point level — combining multilingual OCR, LLMs and a novel region-matching algorithm, with the new MGVG benchmark.

arXiv PDF Code ResearchGate
ICDAR '24 2024 10 cites

SPRINT: Script-agnostic Structure Recognition in Tables

Dhruv Kudale, Badri Vishal Kasuba, Venkatapathy Subramanian, Parag Chaudhuri, Ganesh Ramakrishnan · co-author

Reframes table-structure recognition as a script-agnostic cell-arrangement task using OTSL sequences. Matches SOTA on English benchmarks with lower latency while improving non-English structure recognition by an absolute +11.12%, and releases the MUSTARD dataset (1,428 tables, 13 scripts).

arXiv Springer Code MUSTARD
WACV '24 2024 5 cites

TEXTRON: Weakly Supervised Multilingual Text Detection through Data Programming

Dhruv Kudale, Badri Vishal Kasuba, Venkatapathy Subramanian, Parag Chaudhuri, Ganesh Ramakrishnan · co-author

A data-programming framework that fuses classical computer-vision detectors and pre-trained deep models into a weak-supervision ensemble — producing high-quality text detections for multilingual and low-resource Indic documents without labelled training data.

arXiv WACV / CVF Code Dataset

Multimodal Document Understanding

BharatGen · Nov 2024 – Jun 2025

Contributed to a foundational multimodal model for document understanding in Indic documents and led the Vision team for Indic Circulars VQA — driving robust model development and Q&A data synthesis. (BharatGen is India's first government-backed multimodal LLM initiative, led by IIT Bombay.)

Handwritten Text Recognition for Indian Languages
